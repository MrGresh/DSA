# 🏔️ Section 7: Heaps & Priority Queues (Q66–Q72)

#### **Core Concept:** The **Top-K pattern** is the bread and butter of Heaps. To find the largest elements, maintain a **min-heap** of size K; to find the smallest, use a **max-heap**. 
#### For median problems, use **Two Heaps**: a max-heap for the lower half and a min-heap for the upper half, balancing their sizes after every insert.

### 💡 Implementation Secrets
* **The "Top K" Logic:** * To get the **Largest** $K$ elements: Use a **Min-Heap**. If `heap.size() > K`, pop the smallest. Leftovers are the largest.
    * To get the **Smallest** $K$ elements: Use a **Max-Heap**. If `heap.size() > K`, pop the largest. Leftovers are the smallest.
* **Bucket Sort for Frequency:** In Q68, if the maximum frequency is bounded by $N$ (the size of the array), Bucket Sort can actually outperform a Heap, bringing your time complexity down to $O(n)$.
* **Java Tip:** Remember that `PriorityQueue` in Java is a **Min-Heap** by default. To make it a Max-Heap, use `new PriorityQueue<>(Collections.reverseOrder())` or a custom comparator.
