## 🛠 Useful Patterns to Remember

### 1. Fast and Slow Pointers (Tortoise and Hare)
The most versatile LL pattern. Used for:
- Finding the middle element.
- Detecting loops.
- Finding the start of a cycle.

### 2. Dummy Node Technique
Initialize a dummy head (`ListNode dummy(0)`) to handle edge cases easily, such as inserting into an empty list or deleting the head node. This avoids constant `if (head == NULL)` checks.

### 3. Pointer Manipulation (Standard Reversal)
Always keep track of three pointers: `prev`, `curr`, and `next`.
```cpp
next = curr->next;
curr->next = prev;
prev = curr;
curr = next;
